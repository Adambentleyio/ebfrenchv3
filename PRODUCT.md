# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Students of all ages, from children to retirees, beginners to advanced learners. Located in Nantwich, Cheshire and surrounding areas. They want to learn French for travel, international business, GCSE/A-Level exams, or personal enrichment. Their context varies: busy professionals, retired couples planning trips, parents supporting their children's education, and university students preparing for exchanges.

## Product Purpose

1-to-1 French tutoring and conversational classes. Make French learning accessible, enjoyable, and effective for every student regardless of age or starting level. Success means students gain real confidence and fluency, not just textbook knowledge.

## Brand Personality

Professional, warm, encouraging. Warm and engaging with a touch of professional whimsy. Medium energy. Minimalist luxury with editorial French flair. Speaks as Emma (I) to the learner (you), with French phrases sprinkled naturally.

## Anti-references

- Generic template sites (ThemeForest, Squarespace defaults)
- SaaS/corporate landing pages with hero-metric templates and identical card grids
- Overly casual or cartoonish designs
- AI-generated generic aesthetics (gradient text, glassmorphism defaults, side-stripe borders)
- Anything that looks templated rather than bespoke

## Design Principles

1. **Warmth over polish** — feel human, not produced. Design decisions prioritize emotional warmth over sterile perfection.
2. **French soul, English clarity** — bilingual without confusion. French identity permeates the design but never at the expense of clear communication.
3. **Confidence without arrogance** — expert tone that invites rather than intimidates. Authoritative but approachable.
4. **Whimsy in the details** — personality through typography, motion, and micro-interactions, not decorative clutter. Delight earned, not added.
5. **Editorial restraint** — every element earns its place. Nothing decorative for its own sake. Generous whitespace, intentional typography, purposeful motion.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance. prefers-reduced-motion support. Visible focus states on all interactive elements. High contrast ratios for all text. Rose accent used for labels and decorative emphasis only.
